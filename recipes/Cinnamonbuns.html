Easy Chai Spiced Cinnamon Rolls

A staple in fall desserts, these homemade cinnamon rolls are the perfect balance of sweet and spiced.

Ingredients

Dough

  • 3/4 Cup Buttermilk adds moisture and flavour to the rolls
  • 1 Packet Active dry yeast this helps the dough rise and gives it a fluffy texture
  • 1/2 Cup Granulated sugar adds sweetness to the recipe
  • 6 Tablespoons Unsalted butter adding unsalted butter lets you have more control over the salt in the dough
  • 1 Egg provides richness, and acts as a bonding agent, keepign the dough together
  • 1/4 Teaspoon Salt brings out the flavour of other ingredients
  • 2 3/4 Cup All purpose flour this is the main dry ingredient in the dough

Chai Filling

  • 2 Tablespoon Unsalted Butter
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Cinnamon
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Cardamom
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Ginger
  • 1 Teaspoon Ground Star Anise
  • 1 Tablespoon Earl Grey Tea Ground
  • 1/4 Cup Brown Sugar

Maple Glaze

  • 2 Tablespoon Coconut Milk
  • 1 Tablespoon Maple Syrup
  • 1/2 Teaspoon Vanilla Extract

Instructions

  1. First, heat the buttermilk in a measuring cup. Add the yeast and sugar and mix
  2. Add butter to a large bowl. Pour sugar/buttermilk mixture into the bowl and mix with a hand mixer until butter is broken down
  3. Add the egg and salt. Mix until incorporated
  4. Add the flour and combine until a dough forms
  5. Empty dough onto a floured surface. Knead for 3 minutes and let it rise for an hour in a warm place
  6. Cover with a wet towel, and let rise for 1 hour until it doubles in size
  7. While dough is rising, make spice mix for filling. Combine cinnamon, cardamom, ginger, star anise, and earl grey tea in a bowl. Mix and set aside.
  8. Once dough is done proofing, punch out the air and roll it out to a 12x12 inch square. Smear the top of the dough with butter, top with the brown sugar and spice mixture.
  9. Roll dough into a log, and cut 9 equal pieces
  10. Place the cinnamon rolls onto a greased 9x9 pan. Let dough rise for an hour
  11. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F
  12. Bake rolls for 20-25 minutes uncovered, until edges are light brown
  13. Combine all ingredients for the glaze, mix and set aside
  14. Cool rolls for 5-10 minutes, before adding the glaze
